Disable AI in Firefox & Chrome

🦊 Go to Mozilla Firefox Guide 🌐 Go to Google Chrome Guide

Tired of AI cluttering your browser? Modern web browsers frequently roll out updates that integrate artificial intelligence directly into your user experience. If you want your classic browser back, this guide will walk you through stripping AI functions from your applications on any device.

💻 Part 1: Google Chrome AI Removal

A. Turn Off Chrome's Built-In AI Settings ⚠️ DESKTOP ONLY

Note: This feature is not present on mobile versions of Chrome.

  1. Open Google Chrome on your computer, click the three vertical dots (top-right), and select Settings.
  2. Click AI innovations on the left menu and toggle all choices to Off.

B. Kill the Local AI Download (Gemini Nano) ⚠️ DESKTOP ONLY

Note: Mobile phones do not silently download this heavy background system file.

  1. In Chrome Settings, click System and turn On-device AI to Off.
  2. Go to chrome://flags, search for Optimization Guide On-Device, and set it to Disabled. Relaunch the browser.

C. Stop Page Content Sharing ⚠️ DESKTOP ONLY

Navigate to Settings > Privacy and security > AI features and turn this toggle completely Off.

D. Hide Google AI Overviews in Search ⚠️ DESKTOP ONLY

  1. Go to Chrome Settings > Search engine > Manage search engines and site search [1.1, 1.4.1].
  2. Click Add next to Site Search and input:
    • Search engine: `Google Web Only`
    • Shortcut: `google.com`
    • URL: `{google:baseURL}search?q=%s&udm=14`
  3. Click Add, then click the three dots next to your new engine and choose Make default [1.1, 1.4.1].

📱 Mobile Setup (iPhone, iPad, & Android Phones) ✅ MOBILE ONLY

Open Chrome on your mobile phone, go to udm14.com, and run any random search query. Then, open your mobile Chrome Settings > Search Engine, and select the newly discovered layout under the "Recently Visited" section to make it your new clean default search choice [1.1].
