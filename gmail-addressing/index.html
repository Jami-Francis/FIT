Gmail +  Addressing

Master Your Inbox: The Power of Gmail "+" Addressing

If your inbox feels like a cluttered digital junk drawer, there is a "hidden" feature built directly into your Gmail address that can revolutionize how you organize your digital life. It’s called plus addressing (or "sub-addressing"), and it’s one of the most effective ways to filter incoming mail before it even hits your primary view.

What is Plus Addressing?

Gmail ignores anything that comes after a plus sign (+) in your username. This means that if your email is username@gmail.com, any mail sent to username+anything@gmail.com will still arrive in your inbox.

Common Examples:

  • username+newsletters@gmail.com
  • username+shopping@gmail.com
  • username+taxes@gmail.com

All of these aliases point to your single, main account. The magic happens when you pair these aliases with Gmail Filters.

How to Set Up an Automatic Filtering System

By using a specific "plus" address when signing up for services, you can tell Gmail exactly what to do with those emails the moment they arrive.

1. Use the Alias

Next time you sign up for a coupon site or a technical newsletter, enter your email with a tag (e.g., yourname+promo@gmail.com).

2. Create the Filter
  1. Open Gmail and click the Show search options icon (the settings sliders) in the search bar.
  2. In the To field, type your tagged address: yourname+promo@gmail.com.
  3. Click Create filter.
3. Choose an Action

Decide what should happen to these emails automatically. Popular options include:

  • Apply the label: Create a "Promotions" or "Newsletters" label so they stay organized.
  • Skip the Inbox (Archive it): This moves the email out of your main view immediately.
  • Mark as read: Useful for logs or notifications you only need for record-keeping.
Why Use This?
