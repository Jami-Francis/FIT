DynaTrap Technical Analysis: Indoor & Large Space Models

This page explores the mechanical and chemical superiority of DynaTrap technology, focusing on CO2 production, vacuum containment, and the specific performance differences between the 150, 152, and 1050 models.

Why DynaTrap Technology is Superior to Glue Boards

Traditional glue boards or sticky traps are passive collectors; they only catch insects that happen to land on them by accident. DynaTrap models are active attractants that draw insects away from humans and into the device using a three-way protection system.

The CO2 Advantage and the Ineffectiveness of LED Lights

The primary reason DynaTrap is more effective is its ability to mimic human presence. Mosquitoes and other biting insects use CO2 (Carbon Dioxide) to find their hosts. DynaTrap units feature a surface coated with Titanium Dioxide (TiO2). When the UV light from the bulb hits this coating, a photocatalytic reaction occurs that releases trace amounts of CO2 into the air, signaling a "meal" to nearby pests.

It is imperative to understand that LED lights are often ineffective as an attractant for this specific process. While LED traps are marketed for longevity, they lack the specific heat signature and UV frequency required to trigger the TiO2 photocatalytic reaction effectively. Without the heat generated by a traditional UV fluorescent bulb, the trap fails to produce the CO2 trail necessary to lure mosquitoes from a distance. DynaTrap models specifically utilize these bulbs to ensure reliable CO2 production.

Mechanical Containment vs. Adhesive Containment

  • No Chemical Mess: Standard DynaTrap models (DT150 and DT1050) use a powerful vacuum fan to pull insects into a secure cage where they eventually dehydrate.
  • Hygienic Maintenance: Instead of touching sticky, insect-covered cards, you simply wash the mesh filter or basket with water to remove debris.
  • Continuous Operation: A sticky trap becomes useless once its surface area is occupied by insects or dust. A DynaTrap continues to work effectively as long as the fan is spinning.

The Critical Importance of UV Bulb Replacement

While DynaTrap units do not require "refills" like glue boards, they do require a new UV lighting element every 3 to 4 months (approximately 3,000 hours of continuous use).

Why the 4-Month Cycle?

Even if the bulb still appears to be glowing blue or purple to the human eye, the UV frequency required to trigger the TiO2 photocatalytic reaction degrades over time. Without the specific UV output, the device stops producing CO2. To ensure the trap remains an "active attractant" rather than just a decorative light, the bulb must be replaced seasonally.

Comprehensive Cost Comparison: Washable vs. Sticky Traps

1. Washable Filter Systems (DynaTrap DT150/DT1050): Cost of containment media is $0.00. Maintenance involves rinsing the cage. Consumable cost is limited to UV bulb changes (~$30/year).

2. Sticky Card & Glue Board Systems: Requires physical replacement of adhesive cards every 21–30 days. Annual card costs range from $60.00 to $120.00.

5-Year Estimated Savings: Utilizing a washable system provides a $200 - $400 saving compared to traps requiring proprietary sticky pads.
